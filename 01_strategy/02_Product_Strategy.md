# Product Strategy

**Version:** 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines how AURA transforms real-world problems into intelligent products.

Every product we build follows the same lifecycle to ensure quality, consistency, and long-term value.

---

# Product Philosophy

We do not start with technology.

We start with a problem.

Technology is a tool.

Value is the goal.

Every product must improve the way people or organizations make decisions.

---

# Product Lifecycle

Every product inside AURA follows these stages:

## Stage 1 — Problem Discovery

Identify a real problem.

Questions:

- Who experiences this problem?
- How often does it happen?
- What is its impact?
- How is it solved today?

Output:

Problem Statement

---

## Stage 2 — Research

Study the problem before writing code.

Activities:

- Read research papers.
- Analyze existing solutions.
- Talk to users.
- Collect data.

Output:

Research Report

---

## Stage 3 — Validation

Verify that the problem is worth solving.

Questions:

- Does the problem really exist?
- Will solving it create measurable value?
- Is AURA the right company to solve it?

Output:

Validation Report

---

## Stage 4 — Prototype

Build the smallest possible demonstration.

Goal:

Learn quickly.

Not perfection.

Output:

Prototype

---

## Stage 5 — MVP

Build the minimum product that users can actually use.

Focus:

- Core functionality
- Stability
- Usability

Output:

MVP Release

---

## Stage 6 — Testing

Test with real users.

Collect:

- Feedback
- Bugs
- Suggestions

Output:

Improvement List

---

## Stage 7 — Iteration

Improve the product continuously.

Every version must be better than the previous one.

---

## Stage 8 — Production

Release a stable version.

Maintain it.

Improve it.

Support users.

---

# Product Standards

Every AURA product must be:

- Useful
- Reliable
- Maintainable
- Explainable
- Ethical
- Well documented

---

# Product Categories

AURA products may include:

- Platforms
- Modules
- Components
- APIs
- AI Models
- Data Pipelines
- Research Tools

---

# Product Decision Framework

Before starting any product, answer:

1. Does it solve a real problem?
2. Does it align with AURA's mission?
3. Can we build it with high quality?
4. Can we maintain it?
5. Does it create measurable value?

If the answer is "No" to most questions,

we do not build it.

---

# Success Metrics

A product succeeds when it:

- Solves the intended problem.
- Delivers measurable value.
- Earns user trust.
- Can evolve over time.
